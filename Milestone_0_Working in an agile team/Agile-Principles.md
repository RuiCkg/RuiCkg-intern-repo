## Research & Learn
### What is Agile, and how does it differ from traditional project management?
Agile is a project management process that focuses on delivering value continuously, adapting to change, and collaborating closely with stakeholders. Unlike traditional project management, Agile does not follow a strict linear plan but allows changes throughout the development process.

### What are the core values and principles of Agile? (Hint: Check the Agile Manifesto.)
- Individuals and interactions over processes and tools
- Working software over comprehensive documentation
- Customer collaboration over contract negotiation
- Responding to change over following a plan

### What are the differences between Scrum and Kanban, and when is each used?
Scrum uses fixed-length sprints, defined roles (Product Owner, Scrum Master, Developers), and structured meetings. Work is planned at the beginning of each sprint.
Kanban is a continuous workflow system where tasks are pulled as capacity allows. It focuses on visualising work and limiting work in progress (WIP).

### Why does Focus Bear lean towards Kanban instead of Scrum?
Focus Bear leans towards Kanban because it allows flexible and continuous delivery of work without fixed sprint deadlines. It suits ongoing product improvements and fast adjustments.

## Task
### Summarize the main differences between Scrum and Kanban in your own words.
The primary distinction between Scrum and Kanban lies in how they handle authority and roles. Scrum utilizes a structured hierarchy with specific, tiered roles to drive project completion, whereas Kanban typically operates without formal titles, favoring a model of shared accountability among all team members.
