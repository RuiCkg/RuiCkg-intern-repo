# Competitive Landscape - Rui Chosa
## What makes Focus Bear different from these apps?
Focus Bear is designed specifically for neurodivergent users, particularly those with ADHD or autism. While Tiimo also supports ADHD users, apps like Freedom and Cold Turkey are more general productivity tools and do not target a specific audience.
Focus Bear combines multiple features such as distraction blocking, habit routines, break reminders, and focus modes. In comparison, other apps tend to specialise in one main area. For example, Cold Turkey focuses heavily on website and app blocking, Tiimo focuses on visual planning and scheduling, and Routinery focuses on step-by-step routine timers.
Another difference is the pricing model. Focus Bear offers useful features even at the free level, with optional subscription upgrades. Cold Turkey requires a one-time payment for full access, which may or may not be worth it depending on the user’s needs. Focus Bear may provide more overall value if someone wants multiple productivity features in one app.
Focus Bear also prioritises comfort and accessibility for neurodivergent users, including offering assessments like ADHD or autism quizzes, which makes it feel more personalised.

## If you were a user, why would you choose Focus Bear over competitors?
I would choose Focus Bear if I had ADHD or autism and wanted an app that combines focus tools and habit building in one place. I would also choose it if I wanted a cross-platform app that works across different devices and supports both routines and distraction blocking.

## What’s one feature that other apps have that Focus Bear doesn’t?
Tiimo offers a strong visual task planner that makes it easy to see and organise daily tasks. It also uses AI to help users plan their day and allows calendar imports, which makes onboarding easier. Focus Bear does not currently have the same level of visual scheduling or AI-assisted planning.

## Based on your research, what’s one improvement you think Focus Bear could make?
Focus Bear could improve by adding AI-powered daily planning that suggests schedules based on a user’s habits. It could also allow users to import existing calendars or reminders, making it easier for new users to start using the app and stay consistent.
